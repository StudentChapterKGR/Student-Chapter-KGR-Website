What is Industrial Internet of Things?

As digital transformation has become a commercial priority for many firms, the term "Industrial Internet of Things" has become widely used in the industry. So, what exactly is the IIoT? The Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) connects key assets, advanced predictive and prescriptive analytics, and modern industrial personnel. It's a network of industrial devices linked together by communications technology to create systems that can monitor, collect, exchange, analyse, and give important new insights like never before. These insights can then be used to assist industrial organisations make better, faster business decisions.

The Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) has changed the way industrial organisations work on a daily basis. Whether it's using Industrial AI to detect corrosion inside a refinery pipe, providing real-time production data to uncover additional capacity in a plant, or accelerating new product development by feeding operations and service data back into the product design cycle, IIoT—and the software solutions that power it—are delivering powerful business results.

IIoT is enabling unprecedented levels of efficiency, productivity, and performance by merging machine-to-machine communication with industrial data analytics. As a result, industrial businesses in power generation, oil and gas, utilities, manufacturing, aviation, and a variety of other industries are seeing significant operational and financial improvements. In order to create a world that works, we're transforming industrial assets and systems with focused Industrial AI solutions to make them more predictive, prescriptive, safer, intelligent, and profitable. Digital Twins, learning, living models that integrate domain knowledge and physics with Industrial AI to detect, avoid, and predict crucial issues in order to reveal insights that produce business value are examples of this. Furthermore, the Industrial Internet's remote capabilities enable essential industries to manage assets, develop functioning systems, and collect data from anywhere on the planet.
